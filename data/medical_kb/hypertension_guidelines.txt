HYPERTENSION — CLASSIFICATION AND ICD-10 CODES
  Normal:           <120/<80 mmHg
  Elevated:         120-129/<80 mmHg        ICD-10: R03.0
  Stage 1 HTN:      130-139 or 80-89 mmHg  ICD-10: I10
  Stage 2 HTN:      ≥140 or ≥90 mmHg       ICD-10: I10
  Hypertensive Crisis: >180/>120 mmHg       ICD-10: I16.9
  Hypertensive Urgency (no organ damage):   ICD-10: I16.0
  Hypertensive Emergency (organ damage):    ICD-10: I16.1

PRIMARY vs SECONDARY HYPERTENSION
  Primary (Essential): ICD-10 I10 — accounts for 90-95% of cases
  Secondary causes: Renal artery stenosis (I15.0), Primary aldosteronism (E26.0),
    Chronic kidney disease (N18.x), Obstructive sleep apnea (G47.33),
    Thyroid disease (E05.x / E03.9), Medication-induced (amlodipine, NSAIDs, OCPs)

DIAGNOSTIC WORKUP
  Confirm with 2+ readings on 2+ separate occasions
  Home BP monitoring: preferred for white-coat or masked hypertension
  Initial labs: BMP (Cr, K+, Na+), CBC, lipid panel, urinalysis, fasting glucose, ECG
  Secondary workup if: age <30, resistant to 3 drugs, sudden onset, hypokalemia

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS (All stages — first-line)
  DASH diet: reduce sodium <2.3g/day → BP reduction 5-6 mmHg
  Weight loss: 1 mmHg per 1 kg weight loss
  Exercise: 90-150 min/week aerobic → 5-8 mmHg reduction
  Limit alcohol: ≤1 drink/day women, ≤2 drinks/day men
  Smoking cessation: does not lower BP but reduces cardiovascular risk

PHARMACOTHERAPY — STAGE 1 HTN (130-139/80-89)
  Lifestyle modification x 3 months first
  Initiate medication if: 10-year ASCVD risk ≥10%, diabetes, CKD, established CVD

PHARMACOTHERAPY — STAGE 2 HTN (≥140/≥90)
  Initiate medication immediately + lifestyle modification
  Most patients: 2-drug combination from different classes

FIRST-LINE DRUG CLASSES
  Thiazide diuretics:   Hydrochlorothiazide 12.5-25mg daily, Chlorthalidone 12.5-25mg daily
  ACE inhibitors:       Lisinopril 10-40mg daily, Enalapril 5-40mg daily
  ARBs:                 Losartan 50-100mg daily, Valsartan 80-320mg daily
  Calcium channel blockers: Amlodipine 2.5-10mg daily, Diltiazem 120-540mg daily

DRUG SELECTION BY COMORBIDITY
  Diabetes:        ACE inhibitor or ARB preferred (renoprotective)
  CKD with proteinuria: ACE inhibitor or ARB (reduces proteinuria)
  Heart failure:   ACE inhibitor/ARB + Beta-blocker + Diuretic
  Post-MI:         Beta-blocker + ACE inhibitor
  Afib:            Beta-blocker or rate-control CCB (Diltiazem, Verapamil)
  Pregnancy:       Labetalol, Nifedipine, Methyldopa (ACE/ARB CONTRAINDICATED)
  Black patients:  Thiazide + CCB preferred over ACE inhibitor monotherapy

RESISTANT HYPERTENSION (BP uncontrolled on ≥3 drugs including diuretic)
  ICD-10: I10 + add Z87.39 (history of compliance issues if applicable)
  Evaluate: medication adherence, white-coat effect, secondary causes
  Add: Spironolactone 25-50mg daily (most effective 4th-line agent)

HYPERTENSIVE URGENCY/EMERGENCY
  Urgency (no organ damage): Oral agents, reduce BP over 24-48 hours
    Captopril 25mg SL or PO, Labetalol 200-400mg PO, Amlodipine 5-10mg PO
  Emergency (organ damage): IV agents, reduce MAP by max 25% in first hour
    Nicardipine IV drip, Labetalol IV, Sodium nitroprusside (hypertensive encephalopathy)
    Target organs: brain (encephalopathy), heart (ACS), kidneys (AKI), eyes (papilledema)

MONITORING AND FOLLOW-UP
  Goal BP: <130/80 mmHg for most patients
  Goal BP: <140/90 mmHg for age ≥65 (individualized based on frailty)
  Follow-up: every 1 month until at goal, then every 3-6 months
  Annual labs: BMP (monitor K+ with ACE/ARB/diuretics, Cr with ACE/ARB)
  Referral to cardiology: resistant HTN, suspected secondary cause, end-organ damage

IMPORTANT DRUG INTERACTIONS
  ACE inhibitor + ARB combination: AVOID (increased AKI and hyperkalemia risk)
  ACE inhibitor + potassium-sparing diuretics: monitor potassium closely
  NSAIDs: blunt antihypertensive effect, worsen renal function — minimize use
