DIABETES — CLASSIFICATION AND ICD-10 CODES
  Type 2 diabetes, unspecified:            E11.9
  Type 2 diabetes with hyperglycemia:      E11.65
  Type 2 diabetes with hypoglycemia:       E11.641
  Type 2 diabetes with CKD stage 3:        E11.22 + N18.3
  Type 2 diabetes with diabetic nephropathy: E11.21 (assign only if documented)
  Type 2 diabetes with diabetic retinopathy: E11.311-E11.359 (specify stage)
  Type 2 diabetes with neuropathy:         E11.40
  Type 2 diabetes with foot ulcer:         E11.621 + L97.x
  Type 1 diabetes, unspecified:            E10.9
  Prediabetes / impaired fasting glucose:  R73.09
  Gestational diabetes:                    O24.419

CODING RULES — CRITICAL
  Do NOT assign complication codes (E11.21, E11.22, E11.311) unless the complication
  is explicitly documented by the provider. E11.9 is appropriate when no complications
  are documented, even if the patient has long-standing diabetes.

DIAGNOSTIC CRITERIA
  Diabetes: FPG ≥126 mg/dL, or 2h PG ≥200 mg/dL on OGTT, or HbA1c ≥6.5%, or
    random glucose ≥200 mg/dL with symptoms — confirmed on repeat testing
  Prediabetes: FPG 100-125 mg/dL, or 2h PG 140-199 mg/dL, or HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

GLYCEMIC TARGETS
  HbA1c: <7.0% for most non-pregnant adults (individualize)
  Less stringent <8.0%: elderly, limited life expectancy, severe hypoglycemia history
  More stringent <6.5%: younger patients, short disease duration, no CVD, if achievable safely
  Fasting glucose: 80-130 mg/dL
  Postprandial (<2h): <180 mg/dL

INITIAL TREATMENT — LIFESTYLE
  Medical nutrition therapy: reduce refined carbs, increase fiber, caloric restriction
  Physical activity: 150 min/week moderate aerobic + resistance training 2-3x/week
  Weight loss: 5-10% body weight loss → significant HbA1c reduction in T2DM

PHARMACOTHERAPY — FIRST-LINE
  Metformin 500mg BID with meals (titrate to 1000mg BID over 4 weeks)
    Contraindicated: eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m², IV contrast (hold 48h before/after)
    Side effects: GI upset (take with food), lactic acidosis (rare), B12 deficiency
  If HbA1c ≥9% or markedly symptomatic: consider dual therapy from start

SECOND-LINE OPTIONS (add to Metformin based on comorbidities)
  Established CVD or high CV risk:
    GLP-1 agonist: Semaglutide 0.5-1mg SC weekly (Ozempic) — reduces MACE
    SGLT-2 inhibitor: Empagliflozin 10-25mg daily — reduces HF hospitalization, CKD progression
  Heart failure:
    SGLT-2 inhibitor preferred (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin)
  CKD:
    SGLT-2 inhibitor (eGFR ≥20), GLP-1 agonist
  Weight loss priority:
    GLP-1 agonist (Semaglutide — avg 15% weight loss), SGLT-2 inhibitor
  Cost-sensitive:
    Sulfonylurea: Glipizide 5mg daily (titrate), Glimepiride 1-4mg daily
    DPP-4 inhibitor: Sitagliptin 100mg daily (weight-neutral, well tolerated)

INSULIN THERAPY
  Basal insulin: Glargine (Lantus/Basaglar) 10 units SC at bedtime, titrate by 2 units q3 days
  Bolus insulin: Aspart (NovoLog) or Lispro (Humalog) with meals
  Premixed: 70/30 insulin — less flexible, twice daily dosing

HYPOGLYCEMIA MANAGEMENT
  Mild-moderate (<70 mg/dL with symptoms): 15g fast-acting carbs (glucose tablets, juice)
  Severe (unable to self-treat): Glucagon 1mg IM/SC or intranasal glucagon
  ICD-10: E11.641 (T2DM with hypoglycemia without coma)

MONITORING
  HbA1c: every 3 months if not at goal; every 6 months if stable at goal
  Annual: lipid panel, BMP (eGFR, K+), urine albumin-creatinine ratio, foot exam, eye exam
  Blood pressure target: <130/80 mmHg
  Statin therapy: all T2DM patients aged 40-75 regardless of baseline LDL

COMPLICATION SCREENING
  Nephropathy: urine ACR annually; GFR annually; ACE/ARB if ACR >30 mg/g
  Retinopathy: dilated eye exam at diagnosis, then annually
  Neuropathy: annual 10g monofilament foot exam + vibration testing
  CVD: statin therapy, antiplatelet (aspirin 75-162mg if ASCVD >10%), BP control

REFERRALS
  Endocrinology: T1DM, uncontrolled T2DM (HbA1c >9%), complex insulin regimens
  Ophthalmology: annual dilated eye exam
  Podiatry: foot complications, neuropathy, peripheral arterial disease
  Diabetes education: newly diagnosed, medication changes, poor self-management
