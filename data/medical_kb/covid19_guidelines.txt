COVID-19 Clinical Guidelines (CDC Summary)
==========================================

DIAGNOSIS
---------
COVID-19 is confirmed by positive NAAT or antigen test. Rapid antigen tests have
high specificity but lower sensitivity; negative results in symptomatic patients
with high pre-test probability should be confirmed by NAAT.

Common symptoms: fever, cough, fatigue, sore throat, myalgia, headache, loss of
taste/smell, shortness of breath. Onset typically 2–14 days after exposure.

ICD-10 CODES
------------
U07.1  — COVID-19, virus identified (confirmed by test)
U07.2  — COVID-19, virus not identified (clinical diagnosis, no test)
Z20.822 — Contact with and (suspected) exposure to COVID-19
Z86.16  — Personal history of COVID-19

SEVERITY CLASSIFICATION
-----------------------
Mild:     Symptoms without shortness of breath or hypoxia
Moderate: SpO2 >= 94% on room air; evidence of lower respiratory disease
Severe:   SpO2 < 94% on room air; RR > 30; PaO2/FiO2 < 300
Critical: Respiratory failure, septic shock, multi-organ dysfunction

HIGH-RISK FEATURES (for severe disease)
----------------------------------------
Age >= 65, BMI >= 35, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, cardiovascular disease,
immunocompromised state, pregnancy, active cancer treatment.

ANTIVIRAL TREATMENT
-------------------
Nirmatrelvir/ritonavir (Paxlovid):
  - Indication: Mild-moderate COVID-19, high risk of progression, within 5 days of symptom onset
  - Dose: 300mg nirmatrelvir + 100mg ritonavir BID x 5 days
  - Contraindications: severe renal impairment (eGFR <30), severe hepatic impairment
  - Drug interactions: strong CYP3A inducers (rifampin, carbamazepine); check full interaction list

Remdesivir (Veklury):
  - Indication: Hospitalized patients or high-risk outpatients within 7 days of symptom onset
  - Dose: 200mg IV on Day 1, then 100mg IV Days 2–3 (outpatient); 5-day inpatient course

Molnupiravir (Lagevrio):
  - Alternative when Paxlovid not appropriate
  - Dose: 800mg BID x 5 days, within 5 days of symptom onset
  - Not for use in pregnancy

SUPPORTIVE CARE
---------------
- Antipyretics: Acetaminophen 500–1000mg q4–6h PRN (preferred) or Ibuprofen 400mg q6h PRN
- Hydration: encourage oral fluids
- Rest
- Pulse oximetry monitoring for high-risk patients

ISOLATION
---------
- Isolate for minimum 5 days from symptom onset
- Mask around others through Day 10
- Return to normal activities when afebrile x 24h and improving symptoms

RETURN PRECAUTIONS
------------------
Instruct patients to return or call if:
- SpO2 drops below 94%
- Shortness of breath or chest pain develops
- Symptoms significantly worsen after initial improvement
- Unable to tolerate oral fluids
