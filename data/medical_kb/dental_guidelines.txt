2. CLINICAL ASSESSMENT — DENTAL EXAMINATION
================================================================================

SUBJECTIVE (Chief Complaint)
- Pain characteristics: onset, duration, intensity (0-10 VAS), quality (sharp/dull/throbbing/constant/intermittent)
- Thermal sensitivity: hot vs cold, lingering vs immediate resolution
- Spontaneous pain: present = irreversible pulpitis or necrosis
- Swelling location: localized vs diffuse, intraoral vs extraoral
- Previous dental treatment on affected tooth
- Medical history relevant to dental care: bleeding disorders, bisphosphonate use, immunosuppression, diabetes, cardiac conditions (endocarditis risk)
- Allergies: especially penicillin, NSAIDs, latex

OBJECTIVE (Clinical & Radiographic Findings)
- Extraoral: facial asymmetry, lymphadenopathy, trismus
- Intraoral: soft tissue, mucosa, swelling, sinus tracts (fistula)
- Tooth: mobility (Grade 0-3), color change, fracture line, caries depth
- Periodontal: probing depths (mm), bleeding on probing, furcation involvement (Class I/II/III), recession
- Pulp vitality tests:
  * Cold test (Endo-Ice): normal = brief, transient; abnormal = no response (necrosis) or lingering (irreversible pulpitis)
  * Electric pulp test (EPT)
  * Percussion test: positive = apical periodontitis or abscess
  * Palpation test: positive = periapical pathology
- Radiographic: periapical radiograph, bitewing, CBCT when indicated
  * Caries depth relative to pulp
  * Periapical lucency = periapical pathology
  * Bone loss pattern (horizontal vs vertical)
  * Root morphology, canal calcification

3. TREATMENT PROTOCOLS
================================================================================

DENTAL CARIES
- Enamel only (K02.51/K02.61): Fluoride varnish, remineralization, sealants, monitor
- Dentin (K02.52/K02.62): Composite resin restoration or amalgam
- Pulp involvement (K02.53/K02.63): Root canal therapy (RCT) or extraction

REVERSIBLE PULPITIS (K04.01)
- Remove caries, place sedative dressing (IRM/ZOE), assess 6-8 weeks
- If resolved → definitive restoration
- If progresses to irreversible → RCT

IRREVERSIBLE PULPITIS / PULP NECROSIS (K04.02, K04.1)
- Root canal therapy (RCT): pulpectomy, canal shaping, obturation
- Alternative: extraction with or without implant/bridge planning
- Antibiotics NOT indicated unless systemic involvement (fever, cellulitis, trismus)

PERIAPICAL ABSCESS (K04.7)
- Incision and drainage (I&D) if fluctuant swelling
- RCT or extraction of offending tooth
- Antibiotics ONLY if:
  * Systemic signs: fever >101°F, malaise, cervical lymphadenopathy
  * Cellulitis (diffuse swelling, no fluctuance)
  * Immunocompromised patient
  * Rapid progression or trismus

PERIODONTAL DISEASE
- Gingivitis (K05.00/K05.10): Oral hygiene instruction, scaling and root planing (SRP), chlorhexidine 0.12% rinse
- Mild-Moderate Periodontitis: Full-mouth SRP, re-evaluation at 4-6 weeks
- Severe Periodontitis: SRP + periodontal surgery consult
- Maintenance: every 3-4 months periodontal recall

DRY SOCKET / ALVEOLAR OSTEITIS (K10.3)
- Onset 2-5 days post-extraction, severe throbbing pain, empty socket
- Treatment: gentle irrigation, alvogyl/iodoform packing, analgesics
- NO antibiotics unless secondary infection present

TMJ DISORDERS (M26.6x)
- Conservative: soft diet, NSAIDs, heat/ice, jaw exercises, occlusal splint
- Referral: oral surgery or orofacial pain specialist for refractory cases

4. PHARMACOLOGY — DENTAL PRESCRIBING
================================================================================

ANALGESICS (first-line for dental pain)
- Ibuprofen 400-600mg PO q6-8h with food (max 2400mg/day) — preferred, anti-inflammatory
- Acetaminophen 500-1000mg PO q6h (max 4g/day) — for NSAID contraindications
- Combination: Ibuprofen 400mg + Acetaminophen 500mg alternating q3h — superior to opioids for acute dental pain
- Avoid opioids as first-line for dental pain per ADA 2023 guidelines

ANTIBIOTICS (dental indications)
First-line:
- Amoxicillin 500mg PO TID x 3-7 days
- Amoxicillin-clavulanate (Augmentin) 875mg PO BID x 5-7 days (for severe/refractory)

Penicillin allergy:
- Azithromycin 500mg PO on day 1, then 250mg PO QD x 4 days
- Clindamycin 300mg PO TID x 3-7 days (caution: C. difficile risk)
- Metronidazole 500mg PO TID x 7 days (often combined with amoxicillin for anaerobic coverage)

Antibiotic prophylaxis (before dental procedures):
- Indicated for: prosthetic cardiac valves, history of infective endocarditis, cardiac transplant with valvulopathy, certain congenital heart disease
- Amoxicillin 2g PO 30-60 min before procedure
- Penicillin allergy: Azithromycin 500mg or Clindamycin 600mg PO 30-60 min before

LOCAL ANESTHESIA
- Lidocaine 2% with epinephrine 1:100,000 — standard, 1-2 carpules
- Articaine 4% with epinephrine 1:100,000 — better tissue penetration
- Mepivacaine 3% (no epinephrine) — cardiac patients, pregnancy
- Maximum doses: Lidocaine 4.4mg/kg (max 300mg); Articaine 7mg/kg (max 500mg)

TOPICAL AGENTS
- Chlorhexidine gluconate 0.12% rinse BID — periodontal, post-surgical
- Benzocaine 20% topical — pre-injection anesthesia

5. HIGH-RISK FLAGS IN DENTAL PATIENTS
================================================================================

SYSTEMIC FLAGS
- Airway compromise: trismus, floor of mouth swelling, dysphagia → EMERGENCY, ER referral
- Ludwig's angina: bilateral submandibular/sublingual space infection → life-threatening, IV antibiotics + surgical drainage
- Cavernous sinus thrombosis risk: maxillary posterior tooth infection spreading superiorly
- Uncontrolled bleeding: pre-existing coagulopathy, anticoagulants (warfarin, NOACs)
- Bisphosphonate use (IV > oral): risk of medication-related osteonecrosis of jaw (MRONJ) — avoid extractions if possible
- Uncontrolled diabetes: increased infection risk, poor healing
- Immunocompromised: HIV, chemotherapy, immunosuppressants — aggressive management
- Pregnancy: avoid elective procedures in first trimester; safe window is second trimester

DENTAL EMERGENCY FLAGS
- Facial space infection (cellulitis): requires immediate antibiotic therapy and possible hospital admission
- Dental trauma with avulsion: reimplant within 30 min for best prognosis; store in milk or saline
- Hemorrhage post-extraction: local pressure, hemostatic agents, sutures
- Acute necrotizing ulcerative gingivitis (ANUG): fever, malaise, punched-out interdental papillae — Metronidazole + debridement

6. DENTAL SOAP NOTE STRUCTURE
================================================================================

SUBJECTIVE
- Chief complaint (tooth number using Universal Numbering System #1-32)
- Pain description: onset, quality, duration, aggravating/relieving factors, thermal response
- Relevant medical and dental history

OBJECTIVE
- Vital signs if relevant (BP for vasoconstrictors, temperature for systemic infection)
- Extraoral/intraoral exam findings
- Tooth-specific findings: probing depths, mobility, percussion/palpation response, vitality test results
- Radiographic interpretation

ASSESSMENT
- Primary diagnosis with ICD-10-CM code
- Differential diagnosis
- Tooth prognosis (good/fair/poor/hopeless)

PLAN
- Immediate treatment performed (e.g., I&D, pulpectomy, extraction)
- Prescriptions: drug name, dose, route, frequency, duration
- Follow-up treatment plan (e.g., RCT completion, crown, implant)
- Referrals if needed (oral surgery, periodontist, endodontist)
- Patient education: oral hygiene, post-op instructions

FOLLOW-UP
- 1-week follow-up for acute infections
- 4-6 weeks for pulp treatment assessment
- 3-4 months periodontal maintenance
- Return precautions: worsening swelling, difficulty breathing/swallowing, fever → ER immediately
