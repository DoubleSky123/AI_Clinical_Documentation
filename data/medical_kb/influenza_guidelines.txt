Influenza Clinical Guidelines (CDC Summary)
===========================================

DIAGNOSIS
---------
Influenza is confirmed by rapid influenza diagnostic tests (RIDTs), rapid molecular
assays, or RT-PCR. Rapid antigen tests have lower sensitivity (50–70%); clinical
judgment is important when results are negative in high-prevalence settings.

Typical symptoms: acute onset fever, chills, myalgia, headache, malaise, cough,
sore throat. Nasal congestion common in children. GI symptoms more prominent in
children (Type B).

ICD-10 CODES
------------
J09.X2  — Influenza due to identified novel influenza A virus, with other respiratory manifestations
J10.1   — Influenza due to other identified influenza virus with other respiratory manifestations
J10.00  — Influenza due to other influenza virus with unspecified type of pneumonia
J11.1   — Influenza due to unidentified influenza virus with other respiratory manifestations
J11.00  — Influenza with unidentified pneumonia

HIGH-RISK GROUPS
----------------
Age >= 65 or < 2 years, pregnancy, chronic pulmonary/cardiovascular/renal/hepatic/
neurologic disease, immunosuppression, morbid obesity (BMI >= 40), residents of
long-term care facilities.

ANTIVIRAL TREATMENT
-------------------
Oseltamivir (Tamiflu):
  - Indication: Confirmed or suspected flu in high-risk patients; start within 48h of symptom onset
  - Adult dose: 75mg BID x 5 days
  - Pediatric dose: weight-based (see prescribing info)
  - Renal adjustment: CrCl 10–30 mL/min → 30mg BID

Zanamivir (Relenza):
  - Inhaled; 10mg (2 inhalations) BID x 5 days
  - Avoid in patients with underlying airway disease

Baloxavir marboxil (Xofluza):
  - Single oral dose: 40mg (<80kg) or 80mg (>=80kg)
  - Within 48h of symptom onset
  - Not for pregnant/breastfeeding women

SUPPORTIVE CARE
---------------
- Antipyretics: Acetaminophen or Ibuprofen (avoid aspirin in children — Reye syndrome risk)
- Hydration and rest
- Cough suppressants PRN

RETURN PRECAUTIONS
------------------
- Shortness of breath or chest pain
- Confusion or altered mental status
- Persistent vomiting / inability to tolerate fluids
- Symptoms improve then return with fever and worsening cough
