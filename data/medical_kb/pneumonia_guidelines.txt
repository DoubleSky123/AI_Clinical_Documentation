COMMUNITY-ACQUIRED PNEUMONIA (CAP) — DIAGNOSIS
ICD-10: J18.9 (unspecified), J18.1 (lobar), J15.9 (unspecified bacterial), J12.9 (viral)
Diagnosis requires: new pulmonary infiltrate on imaging PLUS at least two of:
  - Fever >38°C or hypothermia <36°C
  - Purulent sputum or change in sputum character
  - Leukocytosis >10,000/μL or leukopenia <4,000/μL
  - Cough, dyspnea, pleuritic chest pain, new crackles on auscultation

SEVERITY ASSESSMENT — PSI / CURB-65
CURB-65 Score (1 point each):
  C = Confusion (new onset)
  U = Urea >7 mmol/L (BUN >19 mg/dL)
  R = Respiratory rate ≥30/min
  B = Blood pressure <90 systolic or ≤60 diastolic
  65 = Age ≥65 years
  Score 0-1: Low severity — outpatient treatment appropriate
  Score 2:   Moderate — consider hospital admission
  Score ≥3:  Severe — hospital admission required; ICU if score ≥5

HIGH-RISK FEATURES REQUIRING HOSPITALIZATION
  - SpO2 <94% on room air
  - Respiratory rate >30 breaths/min
  - Bilateral or multilobar involvement
  - Sepsis or septic shock
  - Immunocompromised (HIV, transplant, chemotherapy, high-dose steroids)
  - Age ≥65 with comorbidities
  - Failed outpatient therapy after 72 hours

OUTPATIENT TREATMENT (Low severity, no comorbidities)
  First-line: Amoxicillin 500mg PO TID x 5 days
  Atypical coverage: Azithromycin 500mg day 1, then 250mg days 2-5
  PCN allergy: Doxycycline 100mg PO BID x 5 days
  Combination: Amoxicillin + Azithromycin (if atypical suspected)

OUTPATIENT TREATMENT (With comorbidities: COPD, diabetes, heart/renal/liver disease)
  Respiratory fluoroquinolone: Levofloxacin 750mg PO daily x 5 days
  OR: Moxifloxacin 400mg PO daily x 5 days
  OR: Beta-lactam + Macrolide combination

INPATIENT TREATMENT (Non-ICU)
  Beta-lactam (Ampicillin-sulbactam, Ceftriaxone, or Cefotaxime)
  PLUS Macrolide (Azithromycin 500mg IV/PO daily)
  OR Respiratory fluoroquinolone monotherapy (Levofloxacin 750mg IV/PO)
  Duration: 5 days minimum; extend if no clinical improvement

INPATIENT TREATMENT (ICU)
  Beta-lactam (Ceftriaxone 1g IV q24h or Ampicillin-sulbactam 3g IV q6h)
  PLUS Azithromycin 500mg IV daily OR Fluoroquinolone
  If Pseudomonas risk: Anti-pseudomonal beta-lactam (Piperacillin-tazobactam 4.5g IV q6h)

ATYPICAL PATHOGENS (Mycoplasma, Chlamydophila, Legionella)
  Macrolide or Doxycycline coverage required
  Legionella urinary antigen test if: severe CAP, travel, legionella outbreak
  ICD-10 Legionella pneumonia: A48.1

ASPIRATION PNEUMONIA
  ICD-10: J69.0
  Risk factors: dysphagia, altered consciousness, GERD, alcohol use
  Treatment: Amoxicillin-clavulanate 875/125mg PO BID x 7 days
  Or Clindamycin 300mg PO TID (anaerobic coverage)

FOLLOW-UP AND MONITORING
  Clinical response expected within 48-72 hours
  Repeat CXR at 6-8 weeks if: smoker, age >50, to rule out underlying malignancy
  Blood cultures: obtain before antibiotics in hospitalized patients
  Procalcitonin: useful for antibiotic stewardship decisions

PREVENTION
  Pneumococcal vaccine (PCV15 or PCV20): all adults ≥65, high-risk younger adults
  Influenza vaccine: annual, reduces secondary bacterial pneumonia risk
  Smoking cessation counseling: ICD-10 Z87.891 (history of tobacco use)
